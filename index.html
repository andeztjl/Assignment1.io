HISTORY

The Railway Station in Bukit Timah was originally constructed to serve as one of the 5 railway stations that made up the Singapore-Kuala Lumpur railyway route. The entire line spans across an entire distance of 950km, and it starts at Butterworth of Penang. It stretches across the entire north to south region of Singapore, through places such as Kranji and Jurong/Clementi. However, the station was closed down when the Malaysian-operated railway service in Singapore ceased operations in July 2011. After public calls, the Singapore Government announced its plans to preserve the history of these railway tracks who got decommissioned as art pf the Rail Corridor plan, becoming a part of Singapore's geological history!

SIGHTS TO SEE

There are certain things to look out for when you visit Railway Mall, but the most iconic one of them all has to be the Railway Tracks which is visible to the naked eye even when you're far away. You can get onto these tracks by using the passageways on the side roads leading up to these tracks. You'll see many people coming either from the tracks or going towards these tracks as the path leads towards Bukit Timah Nature Reserve as well. Many couples like to take their engagement and wedding photos under the beautiful and retro-looking scenery, while others like to use the background as a good backdrop for their Outfit of the Day shots. It is a mesmerising place to be in (albeit the many mosquito bites you get along the way), and we are lucky enough to be standing on a piece of history in Singapore's folklore!

That's it for the webpage on Railway Mall! It's just a stone's throw away from Hillview MRT (via Exit A), so do take some time to visit this wonderful place!
